# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

EU DECIDO is an experimental civic technology project exploring democratic participation, open collaboration, and responsible artificial intelligence.

We are committed to creating an environment that is:
- respectful,
- inclusive,
- constructive,
- and intellectually open.

Participation in this project should contribute positively to both the technical community and the broader democratic discussion.

---

# Expected Behavior

Contributors and participants are expected to:

- communicate respectfully,
- engage constructively with differing opinions,
- focus on ideas rather than personal attacks,
- provide criticism in good faith,
- and collaborate transparently.

We encourage:
- thoughtful debate,
- technical rigor,
- ethical reflection,
- and cooperative problem solving.

---

# Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated:

- harassment or intimidation,
- hate speech or discrimination,
- personal attacks,
- doxxing or privacy violations,
- deliberate misinformation,
- trolling or bad-faith disruption,
- spam or malicious activity,
- and attempts to sabotage discussions or development.

This applies across:
- Issues,
- Pull Requests,
- Discussions,
- community forums,
- and all project communication channels.

---

# Political & Ideological Diversity

EU DECIDO discusses themes related to democracy, governance and civic participation.

Contributors may hold different political, philosophical or ideological perspectives.

Respectful disagreement is acceptable and encouraged.

Hostility, extremism, manipulation or dehumanization of others is not.

---

# Responsible AI Principles

As part of this project, contributors are encouraged to support:

- transparency,
- explainability,
- privacy protection,
- auditability,
- human oversight,
- and ethical technological development.

AI systems should assist democratic participation, not manipulate or replace human agency.

---

# Security & Sensitive Systems

Because the project may involve:
- voting systems,
- identity mechanisms,
- authentication,
- and AI governance components,

contributors should act responsibly regarding:
- security vulnerabilities,
- sensitive information,
- and infrastructure access.

Do not publicly disclose critical vulnerabilities without contacting the maintainers first.

---

# Enforcement

Project maintainers reserve the right to:
- remove harmful content,
- restrict participation,
- reject contributions,
- or suspend access to project spaces when necessary.

Enforcement decisions aim to protect the integrity, safety and collaborative nature of the project.

---

# Experimental Nature

EU DECIDO is currently an experimental and evolving initiative.

Governance models, moderation practices and contribution rules may evolve over time as the community grows.
